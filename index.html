01 — About

Who I Am

01

I grew up in Hong Kong after being born in India, and I've spent most of my life trying to figure out how things work — and how they could work better. That curiosity is what led me to Industrial Engineering at Georgia Tech. I'm drawn to the space where technology and people interact: smart manufacturing, automation, and systems that actually make sense to use.

I don't have all the answers yet — that's the point of going to university. But I've had the chance to work on real problems: programming an FRC robot's autonomous routine from scratch, building a data pipeline at Harvard, and running usability tests at a supply-chain tech company in Hong Kong. I learn by doing, and I'm comfortable starting from zero.

Sport has shaped a lot of how I think. I was a student-athlete on a soccer scholarship at Black Rock Academy in the US, trained with Kitchee Academy — Hong Kong's top youth football club — and was part of the HK National Tennis Squad for four years, competing across Asia. Playing at that level taught me to lead under pressure, iterate when things aren't working, and rely on a team. Those habits carry directly into engineering work.
